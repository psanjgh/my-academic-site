## Biographical Summary

I am a computational condensed matter physicist specializing in the spin dynamics of two-dimensional van der Waals materials. My research harnesses high-performance computing to bridge atomistic and micromagnetic simulation techniques with cutting-edge experimental data, uncovering the complex physics governing topological spin textures like skyrmions. With expertise spanning GPU-accelerated micromagnetic simulations, atomistic spin dynamics, and large-scale HPC workloads, I strive to provide actionable insights that guide both academic exploration and future industrial applications of energy-efficient spintronic devices.

I have contributed to international collaborations with groups at Argonne National Laboratory, the National High Magnetic Field Laboratory (Florida), USTC (China), KAIST (Korea), UCL, and Imperial College London. My teaching experience includes leading workshops for the Computer Simulation course and serving as a teaching assistant for foundational physics courses.
